Involvement

    National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE): I held the Telecommunications Committee position during my freshman year in Auburn. In this position, my duties were to contact academic sponsors to see if they would like to speak to our organizations in regards to their companies and provide some helpful resources for our members. In this position, I was also able to assist in gathering data for a Test Bank for students to utilize as a studying resource for their courses.

    Black Student Union (BSU): I held the Academic Success Director during my time at Auburn. In this position, I was able to orchestrate study nights for students to participate in and enjoy a safe space for them to do their homework as well as study for any upcoming exams. My committee was also able to supply these events with academic resources, such as tutors, to assist students with any issues they may have about their courses. In this role, I was able to design and model flyers for our social media team to publish on our Instagram page. In these flyers, my committee was able to gather information, resources, and career-ready tips that can help develop our students to succeed academically and prepare for life after college. This role has helped me become more aware of the importance of a diverse safe space for people of the minority in this kind of institution.

    Emerge at Auburn: In this program, students can receive effective leadership development opportunities to help them refine their skills to lead on campus and beyond. This program allowed me to learn more about what it means to be a leader along with some key traits that can aid me in helping those around me. It was a great chance to interact with other students at Auburn as well as network with faculty and staff members.

    Tiger Excellence Scholar's Program: This scholarship program is designated for first-year generational students. My brother and I are the first in my family to go to a four-year college and experience life far away from home. Knowing very little about college life, coming to Auburn was a different atmosphere for me so joining this program definitely helped me get to know more students who have similar backgrounds as me. This program also does a phenomenal job of teaching different cultures and traditions to their scholarship recipients. So, this program has made a positive impact on my views of college now and how to collaborate with other individuals of diverse backgrounds.

    Greater Alabama Black Belt Region, Louis Stokes Alliance for Minority Participation (LSAMP): This program is for students seeking a degree in the STEM field and provides them with some mentorship as well as academic resources to help prepare them for their respective careers. Members are invited to participate in different events such as conferences to connect with students from other college institutions. This was a great way for me to meet people who may have taken similar courses and provide some useful tips.
